Project

Hovering Art Director

Adobe Stock can keep up with hovering art directors

To prove that you can work faster with Adobe Stock, we built a campaign around the person that has more ideas than any designer can keep up with. Hovering Art Directors or (HAD’s).

Famous for micromanaging designers, and taking up most of their time, made an ideal example to both attract designers attention and show them that Adobe Stock speeds up your workflow.

Diving into the artwork from the film.

Made out of over 150+ stock images, the artwork from the film became the key visual of the campaign. A recognizable image that was used across all different media both online and offline.

Webinars to teach old designers new tricks.

A lot of designers watch “how to” videos to learn new tricks. So we took our campaign artwork and turned it into a “how to” webinar series. In these webinars we taught designers how they could make the artwork, and how Adobe Stock integration can speed up their workflow.

The Hovering Art Director Action Figure.

We used designers’ love for toys, to create an incentive designers couldn’t resist. The talking Hovering Art Director Action Figure. The very first designer toy that can give feedback on designs (even if you didn’t ask for it).

Equipped with a motion sensor, and an integrated speaker, the designer toy can help out designers with feedback, using its many lines of pure hovering wisdom such as “Make it pop” or “Move it two pixels to the left”.
Signing up to try Adobe Stock was only one of many ways designers could get one.

Designers shared their own hovering experiences.

To involve designers even more, we crowdsourced hovering experiences. Designers could win the action figure by sharing their funniest hovering experiences with the world.

Poking fun at agency culture worked.

Using a well known industry stereotype made our audience smile and talk. Designers shared their excitement of the campaign on various social platforms.
